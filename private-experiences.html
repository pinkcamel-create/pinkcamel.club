Experiences designed around

Not an escape from life. A way back into what actually matters — with the right people, in the right place.

What presence
requires.

We design experiences for leaders, founders, and families at inflection points — moments that call for focus, connection, or a reset. Each gathering is built around a purpose, not a schedule.

These are for people who need time with the right people in the right setting — without the performance of an event. They exist for what happens in the room, not for what leaves it.

Categories

Leadership Retreats

For founders and executives at inflection points. A private setting and the space to think clearly, alone or with a small trusted group.

Founder Gatherings

Small, curated groups of founders across sectors and geographies. Not a conference, not a networking event — a deliberate gathering around a question or a shared challenge.

Executive Dinners

For leaders who value a private setting and a focused conversation. Twelve people, one table, no speeches — just the exchange that follows.

Client Experiences

Private Events

Milestones and celebrations held in places and in ways that reflect the person or family they honour. Wedding weekends, anniversary retreats, birthdays that feel like a destination of their own.

Our Philosophy

We don't design
escape.

The world has enough places to hide. What's harder to find is a reason to actually be present — with the people who matter.

These experiences exist for founders, executives, and families who need a setting designed around them — not around an agenda. The value isn't in what's scheduled. It's in what happens when nothing needs to be performed.

Example Formats

An orientation, not a menu.

A three-day leadership retreat in the Moroccan Atlas: Six founders. A private riad. Mornings for conversation, afternoons for silence, evenings for shared meals. No fixed agenda — the structure emerges from what the group needs.

An executive dinner in a private apartment overlooking Paris: Ten guests. One question: "What are you building that outlives you?" Three hours at a single table, no speeches, no slides — just a conversation that kept going long after.

A family weekend on the Amalfi Coast: Three generations, a villa that opens to the sea. Days for different ages to diverge, evenings to converge. Designed so everyone can be together without being in the same room all day.
