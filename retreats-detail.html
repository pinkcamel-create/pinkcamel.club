What This Is

Not a wellness holiday.
A return through
all your senses.

You don't lose yourself all at once. It happens gradually — one busy season at a time, one decision made without enough space, one morning when the silence felt unfamiliar. The Serra doesn't fix this. It gives your body and your senses something to remember.

Yoga with bare feet on river sand. Moving freely in open air to music that asks nothing of you. Slow walks through pine forest where the light arrives in slices. Spa and massage on days that ask only that you rest. Meals built around what nourishes rather than what impresses. A fire on the first night, and stars above it.

Each element is deliberate. Together, they form the condition in which something quiet — and necessary — can finally happen.

Who This Is For

A retreat designed
for people who already
belong to each other.

This is not an open group retreat. Pink Camel designs each experience for a specific group — friends who share a chapter of life, colleagues who've built something together, family members who need a different kind of time. Eight people maximum. The intimacy is the point.

You arrive as a group. You move through the elements together. What the land does to each person, it also does to the space between you.

I.
Friends & Close Circles

A group of women, a long friendship, a chapter that deserves marking. The Serra holds what ordinary life doesn't leave space for — real conversation, real rest, real presence with each other.

II.
Teams & Colleagues

Not a corporate retreat. Something different — where the people who work together get to remember who they are outside the work. The elements reset something that agendas cannot.

III.
Family

The kind of time that families rarely take — unhurried, without logistics, held by someone else. A house in the hills, a river, a fire, and the people who matter most.

Group size
Maximum 8 people per retreat. Each experience is designed specifically for your group — dates, pace, and depth curated to who you are.

Day Zero · Arrival

Fire · Air · Stars · Silence
Arrival — the afternoon is yours
Opening circle around the fire
Setting intentions — not goals, directions
Stargazing meditation on the grass
Welcome dinner in the garden

You arrive and exhale. The afternoon is yours — explore the house, the garden, walk the pine trails, feel the air shift as the light drops. Nothing is scheduled until you've landed.

Once settled, the group gathers for an opening circle around the fire. Each person names what they've carried here, what they came to find. Intentions are written — not goals, but directions. Something shifts when words are spoken aloud in a group.

The evening closes lying on the grass, eyes open to a sky most people have forgotten is this dark or this full. A gentle meditation. Then supper together in the garden. The silence here begins tonight.

Day One
The Land
Opens You First
Air · Earth · Wind · Ridge
Sunrise yoga — river beach, bare feet on sand
Windmill ridge walk — wildflowers, valley views
Picnic at the ridge
Sauna — open afternoon
Nourishment session — food as rediscovery
Evening — rest, conversation, early sleep

The first full morning begins outdoors. Sunrise yoga on the river beach — bare feet on sand, the still water in front, the forest breathing behind. The body wakes differently here than it does on a studio mat. No mirrors. No performance.

Then we walk the ancient ridge trail to the stone windmills: wildflowers underfoot, the valley unfolding below, the wind carrying something unnamed. Picnic at the top with the hills spread out in every direction. There is something about altitude and open sky that reorganises what felt fixed.

The afternoon softens — sauna open, the pace yours to set. As the day cools, a nourishment session: not rules about food, but a conversation about what your body has quietly been asking for.

Day Two
The Body
Remembers
Forest · Earth · Water · Restore
Forest bathing — slow walk through the pines
Spa — aquatic session in the pool
Massage — individual sessions
Turkish bath — open afternoon
Nutrition — what you've been asking for
Dusk — free time, portraits in natural light

Morning begins with forest bathing — a slow, sensory walk through the pine woods. No destination, only presence. It asks almost nothing of you, and gives back more than you expect. The group walks together, mostly in silence.

Then into the pool for an aquatic spa session: playful, low-impact, surprisingly powerful. Two parallel afternoon choices: massage, or the nutrition session — a conversation about what your body has been quietly asking for.

The Turkish bath is open through the afternoon. The day is a long act of restoration. The body holds what the mind hasn't processed yet. We give it time and heat and touch.

Day Three
Go to
the Root
Depth · Sound · Stillness · Silence
Morning — slow, optional, unscheduled
Inner work session — gentle, guided
Spa & massage — individual sessions
Outdoor sound bath at dusk
Evening — tender, unhurried

The quietest day. The deepest. The morning is largely unscheduled — sleep longer, walk alone, sit with coffee in the garden. The body needs this before the afternoon.

A gentle guided inner work session in the afternoon: not therapy, not performance — a precise and unhurried space to notice what has been running quietly beneath your daily life. The group holds the space for each other.

At dusk, we carry blankets to the garden for a sound bath under the open sky — bowls, resonance, the valley at night. One of the sessions people describe for weeks after. The quietest day leaves the loudest impression.

Day Four
The River.
Barefoot. Still.
Water · Sand · River · Flow · Joy
Yoga on the river sand — morning
River swimming & boats — the day opens
Free hours — swim, sun, drift, do nothing
Free dance at the water's edge — barefoot
Circle on the sand — closing the day

The whole day belongs to the river. Morning yoga on the sand — the water catches the light, the forest holds the sound, the practice finds a depth it rarely does indoors. Then the day opens entirely.

Swim. Lie in the sun. Drift in a boat. Do nothing. As the sun drops and the sand holds the warmth of the day, music starts at the water's edge — free dance, barefoot on the sand. No instruction, no choreography. Just bodies moving the way they want to in open air.

We close in a circle on the sand as the light goes. A moment of stillness after all that movement. The river day is the one everyone talks about.

Day Five
Weave It
Together
Integration · Nourishment · Space
Morning yoga — garden, slow and attentive
Free dance — movement as gratitude
Nourishment — tools to carry home
Spacious afternoon — solo walks, journaling
Shared dinner — the same table, different people

Integration — the work of letting what happened settle into the body before you leave. Morning yoga in the garden, slow and attentive, followed by free dance in the open air: movement as gratitude, as integration, as a way of saying goodbye to the version of yourself that arrived.

A final nourishment session: not another set of rules, but practical ways to carry this way of eating and resting and moving into ordinary life.

The afternoon is spacious — journaling, walking the trails alone, or doing nothing at all. A shared dinner closes the evening. The same table as the first night. The conversation is different now.

Day Six
Celebration
& Closing Circle
Fire · Stars · Completion · Beginning
Slow forest walk — no pace, no destination
Closing circle — return to Day 0 intentions
What shifted — what travels with you
New intentions set — not endings, directions
Celebration dinner under the stars

The last full day — and the most alive. It begins with a slow forest walk: no pace, no destination, just the pines and the morning and each other. By now the forest feels familiar. That matters.

The closing circle returns to the intentions written on the first night: what did we carry in, what shifted, what are we choosing to carry forward. The group speaks. Something has changed. Everyone knows it without being told.

New intentions are set — not endings, but directions. The evening is a celebration: music, movement, dinner under the stars. The same fire from the first night. The circle closes where it opened.

The morning is unhurried. A last coffee, a last walk to the ridge, a last look at the hills. No one rushes. That is part of the design.

Farewells that don't feel like goodbyes — because what happened here doesn't stay here. It travels with you and with each person in the group. Something was named together. That doesn't unhappen.

We drive you back to Porto or Lisbon airport. You leave with a clarity that doesn't need explaining. As you reach home, those closest to you will notice the glow from within — perhaps even before you do.

How the Arc Works

Every day moves through
three layers

I.

The Body Opens First

We don't begin with the mind. Yoga on river sand, forest bathing through the pines, spa and massage, free dance at the water's edge — the body is the instrument. When it softens, everything else follows. You can't think your way to what the body already knows.

II.

The Depth Finds Its Moment

The sound bath, the inner work session, the closing circle — each sits at exactly the right point in the arc. By Day 3, the body has opened enough for something deeper to move. Nothing is forced. The structure does the work of timing.

III.

The Integration Is Real

Days 5 and 6 are devoted to weaving what happened into something you can carry. The nourishment session, the spacious afternoon, the closing circle — you leave with intentions and a way forward. The retreat continues to work after you go home.

The Serra · Portugal

The land does
half the work.

The Serra is an ancient range of hills in central Portugal — pine forests that breathe, a river that runs cold and clear, hills where the sky turns dark enough to see the stars fully, stone windmills on a ridge that has held wind for centuries.

The house sits in the valley: pool, Turkish bath, sauna, fire pit, gardens, with the river a short walk through the pines. The ridge trail to the windmills takes forty minutes and opens into a view that does something to the chest.

This is not a luxury resort. It is a real place — chosen because what it asks of you is exactly what the retreat requires. The silence here is not curated. It is simply how the valley is.
