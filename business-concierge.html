The frame

You built something.
Now you are running it
while building the next version.

The market is new. The team is new. The demands on your time and attention are not slowing down. And the personal infrastructure — the things that let you actually think — have not caught up with the professional ambition.

A business concierge is not a personal assistant. It is not a virtual assistant service. It is a continuity partner for people whose business complexity is genuinely too much to manage alone — and whose situation is too specific for generic solutions.

Not a service.
A relationship.

We don't offer task management. We offer presence — across whatever arises. The things you would handle yourself if you had the structure to handle them. The things that don't fit inside a normal service because they are too sensitive, too ongoing, or too specific to your situation.

Some clients need someone present when decisions can't wait. Others need the operational weight lifted so the thinking can happen. Some need both, at different moments in the same quarter.

We stay. The complexity continues. So do we.

Business Concierge is ongoing, not project-based. We work with a small number of clients at any given time — because the relationship requires it, and because the standard demands it.

Enquire first. Not to apply for a service. To begin a conversation about whether the fit is right.

Life & Continuity

When life
moves.

Some relocations are just logistics. A house, a city, a new routine. Others are entire lives reorganising themselves — professionally and personally, simultaneously. A business moving to a new country. Children changing schools. A family settling into a new context.

The professional and the personal are not separate when you are in the middle of a life relocation. They never are.

We hold both sides of the move at once. The operational and the personal. What is ending and what is beginning. Because you can't fully arrive at the new place if you are still carrying everything from the old one.

What we carry.

  • Setting up a home in a new context — with the care the family requires
  • Managing the operational side of the relocation so the personal can settle
  • Ongoing presence for the family — school, community, the things that make a place a home
  • Managing what is left behind — property, obligations, the threads that need holding
  • Professional settling — getting the work life functional in the new context
  • Transition support — for the weeks and months when the new place is still foreign
